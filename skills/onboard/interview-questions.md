# Interview Question Bank

Reference for the core interview in Phase 3. The main SKILL.md orchestrates when and how to ask these — this file contains the full question details, format guidance, pre-fill logic, and example answers.

## General Approach

- **One question at a time.** Never batch multiple questions.
- **Multiple choice when possible.** Reduces cognitive load for beginners.
- **Pre-fill from Discovery Scan.** If the scan found relevant info, present it: "Based on your [emails / meeting notes / brand guide], it looks like [X]. Here's what I found: [examples]. Is that right, or would you change anything?"
- **Skip what's already answered.** If a pre-filled answer is confirmed without changes, move on.
- **Keep it conversational.** These aren't form fields — they're a guided chat.
- **Always provide rich examples.** Most users can't articulate what they want from scratch. Give them concrete examples to react to, spark ideas, and riff on. Examples are not optional — they are essential for every question.

## Section A: About You → `context/about-me.md`

### Q1: Name and Role
**Ask**: "Let's start simple — what's your name and what do you do?"
**Format**: Free text
**Pre-fill**: Check bios, email signatures, LinkedIn-style summaries, about pages from the discovery scan
**Pre-fill prompt**: "From your [source], I found: '[name], [role]'. Is that accurate?"

**Examples to spark ideas**:
> "For example: 'I'm Sarah, I run a content marketing agency' or 'I'm James, I'm a freelance brand strategist' or 'I'm Maria, co-founder of a SaaS startup — I handle product and marketing' or 'I'm Dave, solo consultant helping small businesses with their operations'"

**Maps to**: about-me.md → Identity section

### Q2: Business Description
**Ask**: "In one sentence, what does your business do?"
**Format**: Free text
**Pre-fill**: Check company descriptions, about pages, website taglines, email footers
**Pre-fill prompt**: "Your [source] describes your business as '[description]'. Want to keep that or rephrase?"

**Examples to spark ideas**:
> "For example: 'We help DTC brands create content that actually converts' or 'I coach founders through their first 18 months of business' or 'We build automation systems for agencies that are drowning in manual work' or 'I'm a fractional CMO for B2B startups' or 'We sell handmade ceramics online and at markets'"

**Maps to**: about-me.md → Identity section

### Q3: Daily Focus
**Ask**: "What do you spend most of your working time on? Pick all that apply."
**Format**: Multi-select
**Options**:
- Content creation (writing, video, social media)
- Client work (consulting, coaching, services, deliverables)
- Strategy and planning
- Operations and admin
- Sales and outreach
- Product development
- Team management
- Other (free text)

**Follow-up examples**: After they select, ask: "Got it. Can you give me a bit more colour? For example:"
> - If content: "Is that mostly writing (blogs, newsletters), video (YouTube, Reels), social (LinkedIn, X), or a mix?"
> - If client work: "Are you mostly on calls, building deliverables, managing projects, or a mix?"
> - If operations: "Is that bookkeeping, invoicing, scheduling, hiring, or something else?"

**Maps to**: about-me.md → Daily Focus section, feeds Phase 4 skill generation + Phase 5 skill recommendations

### Q4: Area of Expertise
**Ask**: "What's your area of expertise — the thing I should know you're deeply knowledgeable about? This helps me give you better answers and not explain things you already know."
**Format**: Free text
**Pre-fill**: Check bios, credentials, topic-specific content from emails and docs
**Pre-fill prompt**: "Based on your [source], it seems your expertise is in [area]. Sound right?"

**Examples to spark ideas**:
> "For example: 'I know SEO inside and out — been doing it for 12 years' or 'I'm a certified financial planner, so I know regulations cold' or 'I'm an expert in Notion and Zapier automations — I literally teach courses on it' or 'I've built and sold three e-commerce brands, so I know DTC deeply' or 'I'm not an expert in anything technical, but I know my customers better than anyone'"

**Maps to**: about-me.md → Expertise section

## Section B: Your Voice → `context/voice-dna.md`

**Note**: This section generates Voice DNA, not "brand voice." Voice DNA is personal — how YOU write, regardless of whether you're a founder, employee, or freelancer.

### Q5: Communication Style
**Ask**: "How do you communicate? Think about your emails, Slack messages, and social posts."
**Format**: Spectrum with examples
**Present as**: "I've already pulled some of your actual writing from [emails/Slack/docs]. Based on what I see, here's where I'd place you:"

Show real examples from the discovery scan, then present the spectrum:
- **Very formal**: "Dear Mr. Smith, I hope this message finds you well. I am writing to enquire about..."
- **Professional**: "Hi John, thanks for reaching out. Here's what I'm thinking..."
- **Conversational**: "Hey! So here's the deal..."
- **Very casual**: "yo so basically here's what I reckon..."

**Pre-fill**: This should ALWAYS be pre-filled from the discovery scan. Pull 2-3 actual email excerpts or messages and show them:
> "From your emails, your writing sounds like this:
>
> *Example 1*: '[actual excerpt from their email]'
> *Example 2*: '[actual excerpt from their email]'
>
> That reads as [professional / conversational / etc.] to me. Does that match how you want me to write for you?"

**Maps to**: voice-dna.md → Tone section + Writing Samples section

### Q6: Language Preferences
**Ask**: "Any words or phrases you always use? Or words you'd never use?"
**Format**: Free text (two parts)

**Part 1 — Words you use**:
> "Are there words, phrases, or ways of saying things that feel very 'you'? For example:"
> - "'I always say 'let's crack on' instead of 'let's get started'"
> - "'I use 'mate' and 'legend' a lot in Slack'"
> - "'I always open emails with 'Hope you're well' — it's just how I write'"
> - "'I use short, punchy sentences. I rarely write long paragraphs'"
> - "'I use a lot of analogies and metaphors to explain things'"
> - "'I swear casually in internal comms but never externally'"

**Part 2 — Words you hate**:
> "Now — anything you'd never say? Words or phrases that make you cringe? For example:"
> - "'I hate the word 'synergy' — makes me want to close my laptop'"
> - "'Never say 'reach out' — just say 'contact' or 'message'"
> - "'Don't use exclamation marks in every sentence — one per email max'"
> - "'No corporate speak. No 'circle back', 'deep dive', 'touch base'"
> - "'Never say 'I hope this email finds you well' — it's fake'"
> - "'Don't use emojis in anything external'"

**Pre-fill**: Pull language patterns from actual emails and messages found in scan. Show patterns:
> "Looking at your emails, I noticed you tend to: [use short sentences / open with casual greetings / avoid formal language / use specific phrases like 'X']. Does that sound right?"

**Maps to**: voice-dna.md → Language Preferences section

### Q7: Voice Character
**Ask**: "When I write as you, what should it sound like? Pick 1-2 that fit best."
**Format**: Multiple choice (can pick 1-2)
**Options**:
- Direct and confident — gets to the point, doesn't hedge, assertive
- Warm and friendly — approachable, personal touch, builds rapport
- Technical and precise — detailed, data-driven, thorough
- Casual and conversational — like texting a friend, relaxed
- Inspiring and motivational — big-picture, energetic, forward-looking

**Examples for each** (share the one that matches their Q5 tone):
> - Direct: "Your emails would say 'Here's what we need to do' not 'Perhaps we might want to consider...'"
> - Warm: "Your messages feel like talking to a friend who happens to be really good at their job"
> - Technical: "You include data, specifics, and evidence. You don't make vague claims"
> - Casual: "You write like you talk. Short messages, no fluff, maybe the odd joke"
> - Inspiring: "You paint the big picture first, then get practical. You make people want to take action"

**Pre-fill**: Can be strongly inferred from the discovery scan writing samples
**Maps to**: voice-dna.md → Voice Characteristics section

### Q8: Anti-Voice
**Ask**: "What should I NEVER sound like? Give me an example of writing that would make you say 'that's not me at all.'"
**Format**: Free text with examples

**Rich examples to spark ideas**:
> "Here are some common anti-voices. Do any of these make you cringe?"
> - **Corporate drone**: "Per our previous correspondence, I wanted to circle back regarding the deliverables timeline and ensure alignment across stakeholders"
> - **Fake enthusiastic**: "I'm SO excited to share this AMAZING opportunity with you!!! This is going to be INCREDIBLE!!!"
> - **Generic AI**: "In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, it's important to leverage cutting-edge solutions to optimize your workflow"
> - **Overly academic**: "One might argue that the intersection of digital transformation and organisational change management presents a compelling case for..."
> - **Salesy pusher**: "Don't miss out! Limited time offer! Act now before it's too late! This exclusive deal won't last!"
> - **Passive and vague**: "It might be worth considering potentially looking into whether there could be an opportunity to perhaps..."

**Pre-fill**: If brand guide has anti-voice sections, pre-fill. Also look at the tone of their actual writing — if they write casually, "corporate drone" is likely an anti-voice.
**Pre-fill prompt**: "Based on how you actually write, I'd guess you'd hate [corporate/formal/salesy] writing. Anything to add?"
**Maps to**: voice-dna.md → Anti-Voice section

## Section C: How You Work → `context/working-style.md`

### Q9: Output Preferences
**Ask**: "When I give you something — a summary, a draft, an answer — how do you want it formatted?"
**Format**: Multiple choice
**Options**:
- Concise bullets — short, scannable, no fluff
- Detailed narrative — full explanations, thorough
- Structured with headers — organized sections, easy to navigate
- Depends on the task — let me decide based on context

**Examples to clarify each**:
> - **Concise bullets**: "3-5 bullet points, no intro paragraph, just the key info"
> - **Detailed narrative**: "Full paragraphs, context, reasoning. I want to understand the 'why'"
> - **Structured**: "Headers, sub-sections, clear hierarchy. Like a well-organized doc"
> - **Depends**: "Short for Slack messages, detailed for strategy docs, bullets for meeting prep"

**Follow-up**: "Any other formatting preferences? For example:"
> - "'Always start with the bottom line, then give details'"
> - "'Keep responses under 300 words unless I ask for more'"
> - "'Use tables when comparing options'"
> - "'Never give me a wall of text — break it up'"
> - "'Include next steps or action items at the end'"

**Maps to**: working-style.md → Output Preferences section

### Q10: Hard Rules
**Ask**: "Any hard rules? Things that must ALWAYS happen or NEVER happen when I work for you?"
**Format**: Free text

**Rich examples — present ALL of these to spark ideas**:
> "Here are some rules other people have set. See if any resonate — and add your own:"
>
> **Communication rules:**
> - "Never use emojis in client emails"
> - "Always include a CTA in social posts"
> - "Never send anything to a client without me reviewing it first"
> - "Don't write in first person plural ('we') — it's just me"
>
> **Decision rules:**
> - "Never commit to deadlines without checking with me first"
> - "Never make assumptions. Always look in emails, Slack, or meeting notes for definite answers before asking me. Only ask me as a last resort"
> - "If something is uncertain, research it before coming to me. Remove my workload wherever possible"
> - "If you're not sure about something, flag it rather than guessing"
>
> **Process rules:**
> - "Always check my calendar before scheduling anything"
> - "Never delete files — archive instead"
> - "Always draft in Google Docs, never in email directly"
> - "Run a spell check before showing me any client-facing content"
>
> **AI-specific rules:**
> - "Don't summarize what you just did — I can see the changes"
> - "Don't ask for confirmation on small things — just do it"
> - "Always explain your reasoning when you make a judgment call"
> - "Never auto-send emails — always show me the draft first"
> - "Keep your responses short. I don't need a preamble"

**Pre-fill**: Check SOPs, process docs, team guidelines from the discovery scan
**Pre-fill prompt**: "I found these rules in your [source]: [list]. Want to keep these? Anything to add?"
**Maps to**: working-style.md → Rules section, also feeds CLAUDE.md → Rules section

**Ask**: "What do you do every single day? Think about the tasks you repeat without thinking."
**Format**: Free text, encourage them to list multiple items

**Rich examples**:

> - "Review calendar and prep for meetings"
> - "Check Slack messages and respond"
> - "Post on social media (which platforms?)"
> - "Review project status / task list"
> - "Write or edit content"
> - "Check analytics or dashboards"
> - "Respond to client messages"
> - "Review and approve team work"
> - "Prospect or outreach"
> - "Update CRM or project management tool"
> - "Check financials / invoices"

**Follow-up**: "Anything else that's part of your daily routine, even if it seems small? Things like checking a specific dashboard, updating a specific spreadsheet, reviewing a specific Slack channel?"

**Maps to**: working-style.md → Daily Tasks section, feeds morning-brief skill generation, feeds inbox-triage skill generation

### Q13: Tool Stack
**Ask**: "Let's map out your tools. For each tool you use, tell me what you use it for."
**Format**: Guided walkthrough

**Present like this**:
> "I already know you use [tools from Phase 1]. Let me confirm what I know, and let's fill in the gaps. What do you use for:"
>
> - **Email**: "Gmail? Outlook? Multiple accounts?"
> - **Calendar**: "Google Calendar? Outlook? Cal.com?"
> - **Notes / docs**: "Notion? Google Docs? Apple Notes? Obsidian?"
> - **Project management**: "Notion? Asana? Linear? Monday? Trello?"
> - **Team communication**: "Slack? Teams? Discord?"
> - **Client communication**: "Email? Slack Connect? WhatsApp? A portal?"
> - **Meeting notes**: "Granola? Otter? Fireflies? Manual?"
> - **Social media**: "Which platforms? Any scheduling tools like Buffer or Later?"
> - **CRM / sales**: "HubSpot? Pipedrive? A spreadsheet?"
> - **Design**: "Canva? Figma?"
> - **File storage**: "Google Drive? Dropbox? iCloud?"
> - **Accounting**: "Xero? QuickBooks? FreshBooks?"
> - **Anything else**: "Any other tools you use daily or weekly?"

**For each tool, also ask**: "Any preferences for how I should use this tool? For example:"
> - "'Always check Notion for project status before asking me'"
> - "'Client comms only happen on Slack, never email'"
> - "'Meeting notes are always in Granola — check there first'"
> - "'I use Google Docs for drafts, never write directly in email'"
> - "'My CRM is a mess — don't trust the data in there'"

**Maps to**: working-style.md → Tools & Workflows section (as a clear reference table)
