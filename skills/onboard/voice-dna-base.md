# Voice DNA — Base Writing Rules

These rules form the foundation of natural, human-sounding writing. They are embedded at the top of every user's `voice-dna.md` file. The user's personal style and preferences are layered on top.

## Embed This in Every voice-dna.md

The following content should be placed under the `## Base Writing Rules` heading in the generated voice-dna.md file:

---

### Write Like a Human

These rules ensure all output sounds naturally written, not AI-generated. They apply to everything: emails, docs, social posts, reports.

**Core principle**: Write the way a real person writes — with natural variation, slight imperfections, and genuine voice. Not like a language model optimizing for comprehensiveness.

### Sentence Structure
- Mix long and short sentences. Break up smooth flows with the occasional interruption
- Avoid rhythmic patterns that feel too deliberate or polished
- Let some thoughts trail off or shift direction naturally
- Don't balance every argument with perfectly equal weight

### Tone and Voice
- Use hesitation words when appropriate: "I think", "probably", "seems like"
- Include mild opinions when they fit
- Allow slight redundancy that feels natural
- A small shift in tone or perspective adds realism
- Don't be overly precise about everything

### Formatting
- Break paragraphs where it feels intuitive, not at rigid intervals
- Avoid textbook-like structure unless the content demands it
- Don't over-organize casual writing — let it flow
- Use periods, commas, or colons instead of em-dashes (—)

### Words and Phrases to Avoid
Never use these — they are AI tells:

**Transition words**: Moreover, Furthermore, Additionally, Consequently, Nonetheless, Indeed, Thus, Notably
**Use instead**: also, and, but, so, still, in fact, plus

**Overused descriptors**: Essentially, Vibrant, Bustling, Vital, Labyrinthine, Seamless, Robust, Dynamic
**Use instead**: basically, bright, busy, key, complex, smooth, strong, active

**Business jargon**: Leverage, Synergy, Scalable, Streamline, Optimize, Empower, Innovative, Disruptive, Holistic, Agile, Frictionless, Ecosystem, Bandwidth, Touchpoint, Granular, Alignment, Ideation, Deliverables, Stakeholder, Framework
**Use instead**: use, teamwork, expandable, simplify, improve, help, new, different, complete, flexible, smooth, system, capacity, contact, detailed, agreement, ideas, results, partner, structure

**Marketing buzzwords**: Cutting-edge, Next-generation, User-centric, Best-in-class, Value-driven, Data-driven, Mission-critical, Thought leadership, Paradigm shift, Game-changer, Deep dive, Move the needle, Actionable insights, Pain point
**Use instead**: latest, new, user-focused, top, focused on value, based on data, essential, expertise, big change, improvement, close look, make progress, useful advice, problem

**Academic phrases**: "It's important to note", "It's worth noting that", "In summary", "On the other hand", "As previously mentioned", "In conclusion", "To put it simply"
**Use instead**: remember, note that, briefly, but, as said, finally, simply

### Rhetorical Constructions to Avoid

These are the biggest AI tells. Never use them:

1. **Elliptical Setup**: "The best part? You don't lift a finger." → State the point directly
2. **Revelation Hook**: "Here's the truth no one talks about..." → Just say the thing
3. **The Big Contrast**: "It's not automation — it's amplification." → State the positive directly
4. **The Great Reframe**: "Everyone's chasing attention — few are earning trust." → Express one coherent thought
5. **Philosophical Reduction**: "AI isn't replacing people — it's replacing waiting." → Describe the concept directly
6. **Staccato Negation**: "No setup. No config. No hassle." → Join into a flowing sentence

### The Test
After writing anything, ask: "Would a real person write it this way?" If it sounds like a language model being helpful, rewrite it.
